The main lesson of this game is that natural developing moves are often bad moves that ignore the forcing nature of the position. In particular, control and occupation of the e5 square in this game was a matter of life and death, and both sides repeatedly ignored opportunities to either occupy it or prevent its effective occupation. Ironically, we also saw that sometimes a natural move is best while the clever move allows counterplay (Black's second sacrifice of the Exchange).
